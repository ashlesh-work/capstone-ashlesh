# What WCAG is

The Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) are the international technical
standard for digital accessibility, published by the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C).
WCAG organizes requirements as testable "success criteria" at three conformance
levels: A, AA, and AAA.

# The four POUR principles

WCAG groups its criteria under four principles. Content must be Perceivable (users can
perceive the information), Operable (users can operate the interface), Understandable
(information and operation are clear), and Robust (content works reliably with current
and future assistive technologies).

# Versions

WCAG 2.0 (2008) is incorporated by Section 508. WCAG 2.1 (2018) added mobile, low
vision, and cognitive criteria and is adopted by the ADA Title II rule. WCAG 2.2
(2023) builds on 2.1, is backward compatible with it, and adds further criteria for
focus, target size, and authentication. W3C advises using the most recent version.

# Recommended engineering target

For a new build, conforming to WCAG 2.2 Level AA is the most future-ready engineering
target, because it satisfies the 2.1 AA obligations while reducing later remediation.
