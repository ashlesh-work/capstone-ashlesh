# Who Title II covers

ADA Title II applies to state and local governments ("public entities") and the
programs, services, and activities they provide to the public.

# The technical standard

Under DOJ's 2024 final rule, covered web content and mobile apps must conform to
WCAG 2.1 Level AA, subject to limited exceptions.

# Compliance dates

DOJ set compliance dates based on population size. Public entities serving a
population of 50,000 or more must comply by April 26, 2027. Public entities serving a
population under 50,000, and special district governments, must comply by
April 26, 2028.

# Exceptions do not remove the duty of effective communication

Even where a specific exception applies, public entities still owe effective
communication, reasonable modifications, and an equal opportunity to participate.
Procuring a third-party tool does not transfer the obligation: content posted by the
government or its vendors generally does not fall within the "third-party content"
exception.

# What this means for an embedded tool

An embedded chatbot, voice assistant, scheduling widget, or payment component
inserted into a covered government page is treated as part of that page's
accessibility obligation — not as an exempt black box.

# Note on legal interpretation

Compliance dates, covered entities, and exceptions are legal determinations. Confirm
applicability and deadlines with counsel before relying on them for a specific entity.
