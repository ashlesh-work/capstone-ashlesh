# Who Section 508 covers

Section 508 of the Rehabilitation Act applies to federal agencies and the information
and communication technology (ICT) they develop, procure, maintain, or use.

# The Revised 508 Standards

The Revised 508 Standards, published by the U.S. Access Board in 2017 and required for
agencies from January 18, 2018, harmonize federal ICT accessibility with WCAG 2.0
Level A and AA for electronic content, software, and supporting documentation.

# Functional performance and equivalent facilitation

The Standards preserve functional performance criteria — outcomes a product must
support for users with different abilities — and an equivalent facilitation concept,
meaning an alternative design may be acceptable if it provides substantially
equivalent or greater accessibility and usability.

# Procurement: VPAT and ACR

If a product is sold to or operated for a federal entity, the vendor should expect
requests for a Voluntary Product Accessibility Template (VPAT) or Accessibility
Conformance Report (ACR), evidence of conformance testing, and remediation
commitments as part of procurement.

# Note on legal interpretation

Whether Section 508 applies to a specific product or engagement is a legal and
contractual determination; confirm scope with counsel and the contracting office.
