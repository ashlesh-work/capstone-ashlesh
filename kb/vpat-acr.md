# What they are

A Voluntary Product Accessibility Template (VPAT) is a document a vendor completes to
describe how a product meets accessibility standards. The completed document is called
an Accessibility Conformance Report (ACR). It is the common vehicle for reporting how
well a product conforms to standards such as WCAG or the Section 508 Standards.

# When you need one

If you are selling or providing ICT to a federal agency, expect to provide an ACR
during procurement. If you are a public entity or business embedding third-party
components, request an ACR for each major component so you understand its conformance
and gaps.

# How to read one

An ACR records, criterion by criterion, whether a product supports a requirement,
partially supports it, or does not support it, with explanatory notes. Treat
"partially supports" entries as a to-do list, and verify vendor claims with your own
testing rather than accepting the document at face value.
